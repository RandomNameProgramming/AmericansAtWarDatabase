The Seven Years / French and Indian War

Information about various American groups during the Seven Years/French and Indian War

Both sides had Native American tribes fighting with them.

The Alogonquin, Lenape, Wyandot, Ojibwa, Ottowa, Shawnee, and Mi'kmaq sided with the French. While the Iroquois Confederacy sided with the British.

Tribes were forced to fight each other, and frequently switched sides.

During this time period African Ameicans could rarely fight in the Military because it was decided that giving slaves, or free blacks, weapons and military equipment was not the best idea.

Shortly after the beginning of the French and Indian multiple colonies needed extra troops and began inlisting blacks to fill the gaps.

Free blacks were allowed to fight for the British Army furing the war. They only ever held low positions in the army though.

Those left behind frequently took up the slack of the people who left for war.

Men

During the war most Men of age were sent off to fight for either side. Life outside of battle had been calm but also strict.

Women

Some women traveled with the armies while others stayed behind and took care of children.
