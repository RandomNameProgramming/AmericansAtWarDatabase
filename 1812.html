The War of 1812

Information about various American groups during the War of 1812

Uninvolvment

Native American tribes stayed very uninvolved during the war. The only exception is when a Shawnee man, named Tecumseh, was killed.

During the war many blacks fought in both intergated and all black regiments. Many of those that didn't worked to supply materials and other needs.

Men

Those who went off to fight spent much time on the seas and land.

Women

Women stayed home and lived their lives.
